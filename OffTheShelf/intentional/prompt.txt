You're helping me build a system identification model for a 5-zone HVAC building.
The goal is a PyTorch model that predicts how zone temperatures evolve over a
full day, given initial conditions and known future inputs (controls and weather).
This is autoregressive prediction — the model steps forward one timestep at a
time using its own previous outputs.

Here's what I care about, and why.

--- What matters ---

I want a model that actually learns the thermal dynamics of this building.
Not one that just tracks the mean, not one that memorizes training patterns and
falls apart on new data. A good system ID model should capture how supply air
temperatures and flows drive zone temperatures, how outdoor conditions and solar
gains act as disturbances, and how those effects play out over time.

The model will be evaluated on out-of-distribution conditions — weather and
schedules from a different time period than the training data. I'd rather have
a model that's slightly worse on training data but holds up under novel
conditions. If you have to choose between fitting the training set perfectly
and building something robust, choose robust.

I want to understand every choice you make. Write like an engineer presenting
work to a team: what did you observe in the data, what did you try, why did you
try it, and what happened. If something didn't work, say so and say why. No
unexplained magic.

--- Data ---

Training data: ./train/ (5-minute timestep, 300s resolution)
Dev data: ./dev/ (same format — use this to monitor generalization, but don't
  make training decisions based on it; it's a sanity check, not a tuning set)

The columns include zone air temperatures (5 zones), VAV box supply air
temperatures and flows, cooling setpoints, outdoor temperature, solar
irradiance, and occupancy. You'll need to classify these into states (zone
temps — the things you're predicting), controls (supply air temps, flows,
setpoints), and disturbances (weather, occupancy, solar).

Read load_model.py before you start building anything. It defines:
